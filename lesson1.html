English Grammar Essentials

Synonyms and Antonyms

Synonyms are words that have the same or nearly the same meaning.

Big → Large, Huge, Enormous
Happy → Joyful, Cheerful, Elated
Fast → Quick, Swift, Speedy

Antonyms are words that have opposite meanings.

Big ≠ Small
Happy ≠ Sad
Fast ≠ Slow
Synonyms help avoid repetition. Antonyms show contrast.
Prepositions
In: She is in the office. I'll be back in an hour.
On: The book is on the table. The meeting is on Friday.
Into: She walked into the store.
Onto: The cat jumped onto the table.
Above: The plane flew above the clouds.
Verb Tenses
Has: She has eaten breakfast.
Have: I have eaten. They have arrived.
Had: I had eaten before I left.
Linking Verbs
Is: She is a doctor.
Was: The weather was nice yesterday.
Modal Verbs
Must: You must wear a helmet.
Should: You should try this food.
Might: It might rain tomorrow.
Must = strong rule
Should = advice
Might = possibility
Defining Things
A book is a written or printed work with pages bound together.
Photosynthesis is how plants make food using sunlight.
Use simple language and focus on the main idea.
